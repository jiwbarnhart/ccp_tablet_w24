Pets

Hamsters

Hamsters! These were the first pets that both me and my sister had growing up. We each had one hamster.
We enjoyed watching them in their cage/play place. Drinking their water. eating their food. Running on
the hamster wheel. It was all great! Every once in a while my parents would let us take them out of their
cage to play with them. My hamster had white fur with orange blots. My sister's hamster was white with
brown splotches. < 3

My favorite memory with the hamsters was taking them out of their cage to play. Of course this was only
after my sister and I had been good, and under the super vision of my parents.


Chico

Chico was my family's pet growing up. We adopted him in 2003 from the Calhoun County Animal Center.
He was very energetic as a puppy and was well behaved throughout is life. He passed away in 2017, but lives
forever in our hearts. < 3

One of my funniest memories with him was when I was walking him in our backyard. At some point he realized
that he was stronger than me (at the time I was 9 or 10) and that he could pull me around if he wanted.
He started pulling me and I had to anchor myself by hanging onto a tree with my left hand while holding
his leash with my right. Somewhere a picture of that moment is floating around, but I'll always remember it!


Simba

Simba was the first pet that my sister and brother-in-law had adopted and set the naming trend (Disney characters)
Up until Simba, I had never liked cats. I think this is because my uncle was attacked by my grandparent's cat
years ago when I was a kid. He was very energetic as a kitten and loved to bite! When he grew up he became more
conniving. He would climb onto shelves and counters and tip things over and watch them fall. All entertaining
until he tips something breakable. However, we loved him all the same < 3

A fond memory I have with Simba was when I stayed the night over at my sister and brother-in-law's apartment.
I was sleeping in the living room on a blow-up mattress and Simba began to run around me a few times and then
stop and bite my feet. At his age, the teeth felt like small needles! He did this several more times before
going to my sister and brother-in-law's door and meowed until they let him in...at 3 AM


Nala

Nala is technically my sister and brother-in-law's dog, but we all consider her a part of the family.
Much like chico, she was very energetic. One day she injured her knee, and because of it, was not able
to exercise as much. Because of this, she gained weight and became very inactive. However, my sister
changed her diet due to side effects of allergy medicine she was taking. She lost weight and now is back to
he energetic self < 3

A fond memory of mine is meeting her at the SPCA in Kalamazoo, MI and taking he home. She was very timid
at the SPCA center. I could tell she was nervous, so I carried her on my lap. Halfway through the ride
my lap became warm and was getting warmer by the second. I lifted her to realize that she had peed on
my lap! We are on good terms now and she gets friendlier each day.


Jasmine

Jasmine is also my sister and brother-in-law's dog, but of course is consered part of our family.
She was just a puppy when they adopted a year after Nala was from the same facility. Jasmine was
and still is very energetic. To give you an idea of how energetic she is my sister caught her in
the backyard with the other deer. Instead of running around she was hopping around just like them!
It amazes me just how energeyic she is < 3

Jasmine really loves chasing squirels and other vermon. On occasions she has caught them! I like
